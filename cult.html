What is Culture?

Culture is the characteristics and knowledge of a particular group of people, encompassing language, religion, cuisine, social habits, music and arts.The position that the ideas, meanings, beliefs and values people learn as members of society determines human nature. People are what they learn. Optimistic version of cultural determinism place no limits on the abilities of human beings to do or to be whatever they want. Some anthropologists suggest that there is no universal "right way" of being human. "Right way" is almost always "our way"; that "our way" in one society almost never corresponds to "our way" in any other society. Religion and Beliefs

The UAE Emiratis are mainly Sunni Muslims and Sharia (Islamic) law regulates all social and political matters. The UAE is tolerant of other faiths, however and grants the expatriate population religious freedom. Hinduism, Christianity, Sikhism and Buddhism are mainly among these religions. While Muslims can proselytize with those of other religions, it is forbidden for Muslims to proselytize with those of other religions. Muslims are not permitted to convert from Islam, and those who deliberately attempt to persuade Muslims to convert to a new religion have consequences. As an example, there have been instances of people being held and then deported from the UAE for the distribution of bibles. Though bibles are not illegal in themselves, it is illegal to give them out to Muslims.

Arts, Humanities and Popular Culture

The UAE has a long cultural history which dates back to ancient times, derived from many evolving cultures. From the Bedouin tribes migrating across the deserts to the centuries of pearl harvesting along the Arabian Gulf. Today, the UAE is proud of its cultural past while welcoming and encouraging contemporary arts, literature and theatre. There are art galleries, museums and theatres in Dubai, offering an insight into the past and the future of the arts. In 1998, the City of Sharjah was voted the Cultural Capital of the Arab World and houses a local history museum, once the residence of the Ruling Dynasty, at the Fort of Sharjah. Known as the 'Garden City,' the oasis town of Al Ain is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with its natural springs and palm groves. The Al Ain National Museum shows archaeological discoveries from around the area, including stone age and bronze age remnants. Contemporary literature, poetry and performing arts are taking their place in popularity, particularly that of spoken poetry that has its roots in historical tradition. Long regarded as the 'poetry of the people,' Nabati poetry is still very much a part of contemporary literature and has for centuries captured the lives and behaviors of changing cultures.
